# Mosasaurus — Reference Materials

## What this folder is for
This folder holds all scientific reference materials for *Mosasaurus hoffmannii*:
skeletal diagrams, fossil photographs, life reconstructions, museum specimen
images, and texture studies. Everything here feeds directly into
scientifically accurate prompt engineering for Mosasaurus art generation.

## File naming convention
```
mosasaurus_[type]_[source]_[year].[ext]
```
Types: `skeletal`, `fossil`, `reconstruction`, `museum`, `texture`

Examples:
- `mosasaurus_skeletal_nhmm_2014.pdf`
- `mosasaurus_fossil_mnhn_2005.tif`
- `mosasaurus_reconstruction_witton_2018.tif`
- `mosasaurus_texture_lindgren_2011.tif`

Image format: minimum 4K resolution; RAW preferred for photography; TIF for
flatbed or photogrammetric scans.

## Real Animal Analogues for --sref

Use photographs of these living animals to supply `--sref` style references
for specific body regions and surface qualities:

- **Saltwater crocodile** (*Crocodylus porosus*) — jaw structure, tooth shape
  and spacing, and dorsal skin/scale texture. Photograph jaw from lateral and
  ventral angles; close-up of teeth in situ; dorsal hide texture at high
  magnification.
- **Komodo dragon** (*Varanus komodoensis*) — overall body shape and limb
  proportions translated to flippers. Photograph full lateral body profile
  and torso cross-section shape.
- **Sea snake** (Hydrophiinae) — swimming posture and lateral undulation of
  the body and tail. Photograph or capture video stills of lateral swimming
  motion showing full body sinusoidal curve.
- **Monitor lizard** (*Varanus* spp.) — head shape and snout proportions.
  Photograph head from dorsal, lateral, and three-quarter views; focus on
  nostril placement and eye orbit shape.

## Image format guidance
- Minimum 4K (3840x2160) for usable reference
- RAW (.CR3, .ARW, .NEF) preferred for fossil and museum photography
- TIF (16-bit, uncompressed) for flatbed scans of skeletal diagrams
- PDF acceptable for published skeletal reconstructions
