# Quetzalcoatlus — Reference Materials

## What this folder is for
This folder holds all scientific reference materials for *Quetzalcoatlus northropi*:
skeletal diagrams, fossil photographs, life reconstructions, museum specimen
images, and texture studies. Everything here feeds directly into
scientifically accurate prompt engineering for Quetzalcoatlus art generation.

## File naming convention
```
quetzalcoatlus_[type]_[source]_[year].[ext]
```
Types: `skeletal`, `fossil`, `reconstruction`, `museum`, `texture`

Examples:
- `quetzalcoatlus_skeletal_tmu_2021.pdf`
- `quetzalcoatlus_fossil_lawson_1975.tif`
- `quetzalcoatlus_reconstruction_witton_2019.tif`
- `quetzalcoatlus_texture_pycnofiber_study_2018.tif`

Image format: minimum 4K resolution; RAW preferred for photography; TIF for
flatbed or photogrammetric scans.

## Real Animal Analogues for --sref

Use photographs of these living animals to supply `--sref` style references
for specific body regions and surface qualities:

- **Marabou stork** (*Leptoptilos crumenifer*) — standing posture and long
  leg proportions. Photograph full body standing from lateral and frontal
  angles; focus on the stiff-legged, upright terrestrial stance and bare
  skin texture on head/neck.
- **Pelican** (*Pelecanus* spp.) — beak shape and proportions. Photograph
  head and beak from lateral and three-quarter angles; capture the long,
  pointed bill profile and how it attaches to the skull.
- **Giraffe** (*Giraffa camelopardalis*) — scale reference for extreme
  height. Photograph giraffe next to humans, vehicles, or trees to convey
  the sense of towering scale that Quetzalcoatlus shared (~5 m tall
  standing).
- **Albatross** (*Diomedea* spp.) — wingspan proportions and soaring flight
  posture. Photograph in flight from below and laterally; capture the long,
  narrow wing planform and extended soaring position.

## Image format guidance
- Minimum 4K (3840x2160) for usable reference
- RAW (.CR3, .ARW, .NEF) preferred for fossil and museum photography
- TIF (16-bit, uncompressed) for flatbed scans of skeletal diagrams
- PDF acceptable for published skeletal reconstructions
